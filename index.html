At this time, I craft open-source software to keep my sanity in check.

It all started in 2007 during my last year of middle school. I was welcomed home by a UPS box from Verizon, inside it were a dial-up modem, an ethernet cable, AC adaptor, and an anolog telephone adaptor. A few agonizing hours later, I had unfiltered access to the internet. It felt great. Over the next few days, I spent countless hours @Miniclip and @Freewebs building the foundation that I now stand on. From a static one-page HTML with 10 mb of storage to 16 core 4.5 Ghz processors running N-nebular permutations with unlimited bandwitch and storage, the tools I've managed to play around with have humbled me nonetheless.
